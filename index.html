About Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is a major form of Indian classical dance that originated in Tamil Nadu. Known for its fixed upper torso, bent legs, and intricate footwork, Bharatanatyam conveys various emotions through expressions and gestures.

History

The dance form dates back to ancient times and has evolved from temple dances to become one of the most respected classical arts in India.

Significance

Bharatanatyam holds immense cultural and spiritual importance, often depicting stories from Hindu mythology.

Famous Dancers

Some of the legendary Bharatanatyam dancers include Rukmini Devi Arundale, Padma Subrahmanyam, and Alarmel Valli.

Roja Kannan, a celebrated Bharatanatyam dancer and guru, recently performed at Shilparamam in Hyderabad with seven of her disciples.

The theme of the performance was “Gems of Trinity” and featured compositions of the three pitamahas of Carnatic music.

The event aimed to spread the rich knowledge and art of Bharatanatyam and featured precise and detailed performances from the eight dancers.
